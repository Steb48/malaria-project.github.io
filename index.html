Transmission

Malaria is usually transmitted by a certain type of mosquito called the Anopheles mosquitoe. The disease can only be transmitted by female mosquitos, as they are the only ones that requires blood. The mosquitos are actually first infected by taking the blood of another infected person. The blood contains the malaria parasites and it infects the mosquito.

Symptoms

Symptoms from Malaria often are a Fever, Chills, Discomfort, Headaches, Nausea, Vomitting, Diarrhea, Muscle or join pain, and sweating. If severe enough, things such as kidney failure, seizures, coma, and even death.

Deaths caused by Malaria

The statistics from 2021 shows that there were approximately 247 million cases of malaria around the world. Comparing this to the 245 million cases in 2020, the increase is not good news. The number of deaths caused by malaria was about 619,000 in 2021.

Prevention Methods

Antimalarial medication

You can just take antimalarial medication to kill all the parasites and prevent yourself by being infected,

Prevent mosquito bites

You can prevent mosquito bites by staying indoors during their active hours (usually around dusk and dawn). Also wear long sleeve shirts and long pants when going outside.

Vaccine

You can always just take a vaccine for malaria. There's a malaria vaccine called the RTS,S/AS01 that's widely used for children living in sub-Saharan Africa.
